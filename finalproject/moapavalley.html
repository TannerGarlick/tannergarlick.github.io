Moapa Valley History and Attractions

Moapa Valley is in Clark County, Nevada. About an hour drive to Las Vegas. The valley is about 40 miles long and has multiple towns in it. Most locals will either call our town Logandale or Moapa Valley. This is because Moapa Valley consists of 3 towns. These towns include Moapa, Logandale, and Overton. Moapa Valley has a river that runs through the town called the Muddy River. The water comes from a spring and flows into Lake Mead. Many people either that live in Moapa Valley or drive in from surrounding areas will frequently go boating on Lake Mead. Another thing that many people do in Moapa Valley is go off-roading in the desert on ATVs, dirt bikes, razors, trucks, and basically anything with four wheels.

Lost City Museum

The Lost City Museum was built in 1935 by the National Park Service. In the Museum you can find artifacts found from local archaeology sites. There are only seven state museums in Nevada. The Museum has exhibition galleries, a screening room, and a research library. There are also Native American pit houses and reconstructed pueblos at the Museum.

Clark County Fair & Rodeo

The Clark County Fair and Rodeo is a huge attraction for Moapa Valley. Every year in mid-April thousands of people come to Moapa Valley to attend the fair. The fair consists of carnival rides, shows for all ages, variety of food, venders selling random things, animal showings, and the rodeo.

Logandale Trails

The Logandale Trails are over 200 miles of trails that include hiking, horseback riding, and off-road vehicles. There are specific trails that are made for off road vehicles with a variety of skill levels. They have set areas for camping and hiking.

Sugars Homeplate

Sugars Homeplate is a local favorite restaurant. Many people traveling through Moapa will stop here to refuel and get back on the road. It is rumored that Criss Angel is working on purchasing Sugars and turning it into his own restaurant.

The Inside Scoop

The Inside Scoop is another locally owned restaurant. They have a variety of options. You should be able to find something for everyone. They serve sandwiches, burgers, chicken, fries, ice cream and more. They offer dine in or take out.
